Strengths and Limitations

Bragging and Self-Dissing.

Strengths

  • All-Brained

    I do not believe in left brain right brain theory. I love math and I love design.

  • Tenacious

    I love detangling fairy lights

  • Problem-Solving Skills

    Being able to solve other people's problems helps me feel relevent. My creativity seems to help with this.

  • Socially Fearless

    I regularly talk to strangers and quit my engineering job in order to do more public speaking. Spot the middle child.

Limitations

  • Reading Difficulties

    I read about as slow as I talk.

  • Perfectionist

    I love to make art even when it literally does not matter.

  • Distractable

    I can't work while listening to lyrics because I will get distracted wondering whether the song writer's life is going okay.

  • Anxiety

    Without meds, I get stress headaches from playing chess.

  • Vanity

    I feel pressure to succeed in the eyes of others. Unchecked this will stunt my learning.

Managing My Workload

My committment to not being a soggy liability.

In order to be a good worker I am going to try to get good sleep, spend some time outside each day, and not work more than 8 hours per day if I can help it. This will mean it is crucial to put my headphones in and my head down when I need to work. I will use my timer when I get stuck and ensure I spend no more than 20 minutes trying to solve a problem before I ask for help.


When I notice that I am stressed I am going to physically step away from my desk. I will do some breathing exercises or starjumps or even go for a walk and talk to someone. I will ask for help if this happens multiple times on the same problem.


I want to do half an hour of exercise per day. I usually get at least ten minutes of exercise by walking to the bus etc. but I want to do more to make sure my brain is as neuroplastic as possible.

Reflection Pre-Bootcamp: Learning how to minimise stress and keeping my cool will be my biggest non-technical challenge. Battling my brain to try and develop a healthy mindset towards my own abilities so that I praise myself work work done instead of being set in the ego rolercoaster. I would like to learn how to prioritise tasks before doing stretch tasks. I would like to take time to plan before hurtling in.

Seeking Help

War against the unholy union of my perfectionism and tenacity.

I am going to use a 20-minute timer to ensure that I ask for help in a timely way. I will first reach out to my fellow students and then reach out to the facilitators.

Expectations from the Facilitation Team

My committment to not being a soggy liability.

I have a fragile artist's ego so I expect facilitators to not put me down when I ask for help. I expect the facilitators to be patient with me when it inevitably takes me a while to understand new things or read new information. I expect facilitators to respect my need to take time off from work.

Reflection Pre-Bootcamp: Not expectations for the team itself but I would love there to be a coffee machine and a fridge. I expect that the bootcamp team are friendly and will not put me down if I ask for help. I expect the bootcamp team to be honest with me.

Expectations for myself
